Beyoncé to Headline Historic Vocal Tour in Vanadzor, Armenia

Harut Babajanyan Announced as Special Guest

By Lilit Manukyan Arts & Culture Correspondent
2 July 2025

In a groundbreaking announcement that has electrified fans across the region, global music icon Beyoncé will perform for the first time in Armenia, headlining a special vocal tour stop in the picturesque city of Vanadzor.

The event is set to take place on August 24, 2025, in Hayk Square, the city's cultural and civic heart.

The performance will be part of an exclusive acoustic and vocal-focused tour titled "PURE VOICE: An Evening with Beyoncé", emphasizing stripped-down arrangements, live harmonies, and vocal excellence in open-air, intimate public settings. Vanadzor has been selected as one of only four non-Western cities on the tour — a decision organizers say reflects a commitment to cultural diversity and global connection through music.

Concert Details

  • Date: August 24, 2025
  • Location: Hayk Square, Vanadzor, Armenia
  • Special Guest: Harut Babajanyan
  • Tickets: Free with registration
  • Expected Attendance: 25,000+

A Unique Collaboration with Armenian Music Legend

Adding to the excitement, Armenian music legend Harut Babajanyan has been confirmed as a special guest performer for the Vanadzor concert. Known for his emotionally resonant voice and deep ties to Armenian folk and classical music, Babajanyan is expected to join Beyoncé on stage for a cross-cultural duet that producers are calling "a once-in-a-generation musical moment."

"When we discussed potential guests for the Armenian show, there was only one name on everyone's mind. Harut's voice carries the soul of a nation — just as Beyoncé's carries the heartbeat of a generation. It's a perfect synergy."

Jason Lee, Beyoncé's tour director

According to insiders, the duet will blend elements of Armenian traditional music with gospel and R&B influences, symbolizing a bridge between cultures, histories, and voices.

A Gift to the City — Sponsored by Restart Vanadzor Foundation

The event is being proudly sponsored and coordinated by the Restart Vanadzor Foundation, a local nonprofit known for civic engagement, youth empowerment, and revitalization of urban culture.

"This is more than a concert. This is a gift to our city — an invitation to the world to see Vanadzor not just as a place with history, but as a place where the future of music and culture can happen."

Arman Melkonyan, Executive Director of Restart Vanadzor

Restart Vanadzor will be organizing a multi-day cultural festival around the performance, including public art installations, music workshops, youth choir performances, food fairs, and local artisan markets — all centered around Hayk Square and the city's historic theater district.

Vocal-Only Experience: No Backing Tracks, No Auto-Tune

In a bold departure from her usual stadium productions, Beyoncé's Vanadzor set will be entirely live and unprocessed, performed without any digital backing tracks or visual effects. Organizers say this approach is designed to foreground the raw vocal power and emotional intimacy of the performance.

"People know Beyoncé as a performer. But when you strip it all away — no choreography, no lights — what's left is the voice. And in Vanadzor, she wants that voice to connect directly to the hearts of the people."

Marina Hakobyan, Armenian-American vocal coach and tour consultant

Global Attention, Local Pride

International media outlets have already begun covering the announcement, with travel agencies in Yerevan, Tbilisi, and Tehran reporting a surge in accommodation bookings in Vanadzor. The city, nestled in the green mountains of Lori Province, is expected to welcome over 25,000 attendees — its largest cultural gathering in modern history.

Hotels, homestays, and even school dormitories are being prepared to accommodate guests, while the municipality is working to ensure upgraded infrastructure and transportation services during the concert week.

Local youth and student volunteers will play key roles in guiding visitors, providing interpretation, and showcasing Vanadzor's rich artistic history — from Soviet-era jazz traditions to emerging indie bands.

Ticket Info and Livestream

General admission tickets will be free of charge, made possible by Restart Vanadzor and international arts donors. However, registration will be required, and tickets will be distributed in phases, with priority access given to residents of Lori Province and local students.

A limited number of premium viewing packages (including front-row access and meet-and-greet opportunities) will be available via donation to support Restart Vanadzor's arts programs.

The concert will also be livestreamed globally, with multilingual commentary and behind-the-scenes footage available via the Restart Foundation's media channels.

"It's not just Vanadzor hosting Beyoncé. It's Armenia hosting the world."

Local student

Stay tuned for updates on setlist details, security guidelines, and the official cultural schedule in the coming weeks.
